## Heat is not just one thing

The Scoville scale measures perceived pepper heat, but great spicy food lives in the details around that number. Texture, acidity, sweetness, fermentation, and fat all change how heat lands.

## Use it as context, not destiny

Two peppers with similar Scoville ranges can feel wildly different on the plate. A sauce with fruit, vinegar, and sugar can feel more approachable than a "lower" rating suggests.

## What FlamingFoodies cares about

We use the Scoville scale as one lens. The better question is what kind of heat you want: bright, smoky, creeping, immediate, or punishing.
